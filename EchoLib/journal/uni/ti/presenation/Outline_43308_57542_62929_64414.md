## (c) Specific Subject of the Presentation
- Arweave’s Blockweave Architecture for the Immutable Preservation of War Records.

## (d) the outline of the presentation
### 1. Introduction
- Discuss the vulnerability of digital data during wartime, mentioning issues such as server destruction, state censorship and link rot
- Briefly introduce *Arweave* as a solution built to combat this by preserving an immutable historical record 
### 2. Core architecture - Blockweave
- Explain the distinct Arweave's blockweave structure and block creation.
### 3. Consensus mechanics - Proof of Access
- Explain the Proof of Access protocol and how it is integrated in Arweave.
### 4. Case study - A Digital *Noah's Ark* for Ukraine
- Present the practical application of this architecture during the Russia-Ukraine conflict.
### 5. Conclusion
- Conclude by discussing the friction between Arweave's absolute immutability and modern legal frameworks.
- With special focus on the clash with the GDPR's "right to erasure", and the ethical challenges of moderating illicit content on a network where data physically cannot be deleted.
